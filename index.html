About Me

"I engineer brand loyalty and user retention."

I engineer brand loyalty and user retention.

I study a problem that most brands, products, and communities struggle to solve: why do people enthusiastically adopt something today, only to abandon it tomorrow?

Over the years, my work has evolved from brand development and business strategy into understanding the psychological forces that drive consumer adoption, engagement, and long-term retention. My approach combines consumer psychology, behavioral science, decision-making theory, and real-world observation to uncover what happens between awareness and loyalty.

While most organizations focus on acquisition, I am interested in the moments where customers disengage, habits break down, motivation fades, and attention shifts elsewhere. This curiosity led me to develop frameworks such as Temporal Surrender, Mimetic Motivation, and Behavioral Adherence Systems.

My work sits at the intersection of: Consumer Behavior · Brand Strategy · Customer Adoption · Retention & Engagement · Behavioral Design · Human Decision-Making.

Today, I help organizations understand not just how to attract customers, but how to create experiences, systems, and environments that people continue to choose long after the initial excitement fades.

The Frameworks

The Frameworks (How I Operate) · This translates behavioral science into brand and retention language.

01 — The Adoption-Abandonment Gap (Retention Architecture)

The critical vulnerability in every customer journey. The Adoption-Abandonment Gap emerges when rapid, intuitive adoption driven by System 1 decision-making is followed by a sharp decline in engagement as sustained usage demands greater cognitive effort, behavioral discipline, or environmental support. I identify the friction points where initial customer enthusiasm fails to convert into long-term adherence, retention, and brand loyalty.

Business Relevance · Customer retention · Subscription churn · Product adoption · Membership engagement · Habit-based products

↓

02 — Temporal Surrender (Brand Fatigue)

Temporal Surrender describes the behavioral collapse that occurs when the psychological, cognitive, emotional, or physical cost of maintaining a newly adopted identity exceeds an individual’s available capacity. Users initially enter a phase of hyper-adaptation, but eventually disengage when the maintenance burden becomes unsustainable. I design behavioral interventions that transition users from high-volatility adoption cycles into stable, self-reinforcing patterns of engagement and long-term adherence.

Business Relevance · Fitness drop-off · Community disengagement · Learning platform churn · Customer burnout · Loyalty decay

↓

03 — Neuro-Inclusive Design (The Ultimate Stress-Test)

Neuro-Inclusive Design operates on a simple principle: cognitive overload is often invisible until it becomes abandonment. By designing for individuals with limited executive-function bandwidth, such as ADHD users, hidden friction points become immediately visible. If a customer journey, interface, or behavioral process cannot be sustained by a cognitively constrained user, it is likely imposing unnecessary effort on the broader market as well. I design systems built around clear defaults, reduced decision fatigue, immediate feedback loops, and behavioral clarity to improve adoption, engagement, and long-term retention.

Business Relevance · UX simplification · Onboarding optimization · Customer journey design · Behavioral product design · Retention architecture

§ III

Problems I Solved

Selected interventions where behavioral science helped organizations improve adoption, adherence, retention, and trust.

Case · 01 · High-Governance Operations 55% Sustained Adherence

Engineering Adherence in a High-Governance Environment

The Challenge · A high-governance Indian paramilitary unit needed to adopt new operational and behavioral protocols without disrupting critical day-to-day activities.

A field audit revealed three behavioral barriers: Status Quo Bias, Cognitive Overload, and Process Drift. Rather than relying on motivation or enforcement, the intervention focused on designing Systemic Adherence Protocols. New behaviors were embedded directly into existing workflows, reporting structures, and operational feedback loops, reducing the need for conscious decision-making and minimizing additional cognitive load.

Business Impact

Achieved 55% sustained adherence to newly introduced protocols, reduced behavioral drift across operational processes, and successfully implemented organizational change within a highly rigid, high-pressure environment.

Case · 02 · D2C Wellness Retention Retention Engine Built

Building a Retention Engine for a D2C Wellness Brand

The Challenge · A vegan supplement brand was successfully acquiring customers through paid advertising but struggled to generate repeat purchases.

The post-purchase journey revealed high friction costs, a default architecture failure, and a habit formation breakdown. A 45-day behavioral retention journey was designed to reduce friction and reinforce commitment through identity reinforcement, usage-support content, timed behavioral nudges, loss-aversion-based reorder triggers, and automated trust-repair mechanisms.

Business Impact

Transformed an acquisition-focused customer journey into a retention-oriented lifecycle system, increased customer engagement beyond the initial purchase, and established a scalable backend retention architecture.

Case · 03 · Public Adoption Behavioral Blueprint Delivered

Increasing Public Adoption of Blue Spaces Through Behavioral Design

The Challenge · Despite strong evidence linking access to natural environments with improved health, public utilization of Galway City’s coastal and river resources remained low.

The research identified three barriers: Temporal Surrender, Default Architecture Failure, and the Intention-Action Gap. Rather than increasing awareness alone, the intervention focused on changing environmental defaults through walking meetings, social-proof-based messaging, micro-incentive systems, and localized influencer strategies leveraging Mimetic Motivation.

Business Impact

Delivered a behaviorally informed public adoption blueprint, identified low-cost interventions capable of increasing utilization rates, and created actionable recommendations for government and public-health stakeholders.

Case · 04 · Brand Trust & Loyalty Trust Architecture Rebuilt

Repositioning a Commodity Brand Through Behavioral Trust Architecture

The Challenge · An Ayurvedic wellness brand was competing primarily on product ingredients and price, limiting differentiation and reducing long-term customer loyalty.

Two behavioral barriers emerged: the Commensurability Trap and Decision Risk. The brand was repositioned from ingredient supplier to specialized wellness partner through identity-based packaging architecture, community-driven trust mechanisms, and micro-influencer activation using relatable mothers rather than aspirational celebrities.

Business Impact

Shifted customer perception away from commodity comparison, increased emotional differentiation, strengthened trust formation pathways, and built foundations for long-term customer loyalty.

§ VII

N=1 Self Case Study: Applied Adherence

I don't just study behavioral retention; I engineer it in my own life.

Most people begin ambitious fitness, health, or lifestyle goals with high motivation but fail to sustain them once the physical, emotional, and cognitive demands begin to accumulate.

As a competitive endurance athlete training for HYROX Pro Division events and long-distance triathlon racing, I faced the same challenge: maintaining consistent execution under increasing training volume, fatigue, and competing life demands.

Through self-observation and behavioral tracking, I identified three recurring failure mechanisms commonly found in both individuals and brands: Mimetic Motivation, Temporal Surrender, and the Adoption-Abandonment Gap. Initial commitment alone did not guarantee long-term consistency. The challenge was not starting; the challenge was sustaining effort once novelty disappeared and friction accumulated.

Rather than relying on motivation, I designed a personal Behavioral Adherence System built around environmental design and choice architecture. I structured rigid default training systems, reduced reliance on daily decision-making, created immediate feedback loops, and built identity reinforcement mechanisms that encouraged long-term consistency.

The system enabled sustained adherence to the training volume required for HYROX and endurance racing while minimizing behavioral volatility. More importantly, it served as a live testing environment for the same retention and adherence principles I apply to customer journeys, habit systems, and consumer engagement strategies.

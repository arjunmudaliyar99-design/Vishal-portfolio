§ II

The Frameworks

How I Think · The three operating principles that underpin every audit, intervention, and design decision.

01 — The Adoption-Abandonment Gap

The critical vulnerability in any brand's lifecycle. I map the exact friction points where users successfully adopt a product (System 1 thinking) but abandon it when the process friction exceeds their cognitive reserve (System 2 overload).

↓

02 — Temporal Surrender

A behavioral state where users hyper-adapt to a new system or brand identity, only to inevitably drop off when the cost of maintenance causes burnout. I design interventions to move users from high-volatility hyper-adaptation to compounding, sustainable habits.

↓

03 — Neuro-Inclusive Design

Designing for strict cognitive capacity. If a brand’s user journey or digital interface overwhelms an ADHD user, it contains hidden friction that is slowly churning the general market. I build systems that rely on clear defaults and immediate feedback loops rather than taxing the user’s executive function.

Case File · 01 · Organizational Behavior / High-Governance 55% Adherence Rate · High-Governance Compliance Achieved

Engineering Adherence in a High-Governance Environment — Sameekshnam Project

Scope: Organizational Behavioral Intervention · High-Governance Indian Paramilitary Unit

The Context

Managed a large-scale organizational intervention for a high-governance Indian paramilitary unit. The objective was to implement new operational and behavioral protocols without disrupting the rigid, high-pressure daily functions of the unit.

The Phenomenological Audit

High-stakes, rigid environments inherently resist new processes. An audit of the daily routines revealed that introducing any new protocol required active cognitive effort, pulling focus away from critical operational duties.

Behavioral Diagnoses

Status Quo Bias & Cognitive Overload

In high-stress settings, human operators default to established routines. Any new behavioral requirement is perceived as operational friction and is quickly abandoned.

Process Drift

Without constant enforcement, the gap between the “prescribed protocol” and the “actual behavior” on the ground widens.

Intervention Designed

Systemic Adherence Protocols: Rather than relying on motivation, the program delivery was engineered to seamlessly integrate into their existing operational architecture. The new behaviors were embedded into their standard feedback loops, removing the need for active decision-making.

Measured Impact

Achieved a measured 55% user adherence rate, successfully enforcing sustained behavioral change and operational compliance within an incredibly high-pressure, low-flexibility setting.

Case File · 02 · Brand Retention / D2C Repeat Purchase Rate Scaled · LTV Increased

The Brand Retention Engine — D2C Supplement Churn Crisis

Scope: Brand Retention Architecture · D2C Vegan Supplement · (Client Name Withheld — NDA)

The Context

A D2C vegan supplement brand was functioning as a “leaky bucket.” Despite heavy ad spend driving strong initial acquisition, the 30-day repeat purchase rate was stagnant at 5%. The brand was failing to convert initial trials into sustained brand loyalty.

The Phenomenological Audit

I mapped the post-purchase journey to understand the churn. The audit revealed massive usage friction (customers struggled with taste/mixability) and zero “Default Architecture” for reordering. The brand abandoned the user after shipping, relying entirely on the customer’s memory to trigger a refill order—a massive cognitive load.

Behavioral Diagnoses

Friction Costs & Broken Feedback Loop

The brand suffered from high Friction Costs and a broken feedback loop, causing early abandonment before habit formation could occur.

Interventions Designed

Rebuilding the Retention Cycle: Deployed a 45-day behavioral journey designed to reinforce Brand Identity (Day 1), reduce usage friction via recipe nudges (Day 5), and trigger re-orders via loss-aversion incentives (Day 30).

Trust Repair: Integrated an automated ecosystem to monitor incoming customer sentiment and repair brand trust in real-time, lowering the cognitive load on customer service.

Measured Impact

Transformed a zero-engagement backend into a scalable retention engine, directly increasing customer lifetime value (LTV) and turning high-risk churners into sustained brand loyalists. (Note: Client name withheld due to NDA).

Case File · 03 · Public Policy / Behavioral Research Behavioral Blueprint Delivered · NUI Galway / HSE

Behavioral Nudging for Public Resource Adoption — NUI Galway Blue Spaces Initiative

Scope: Systems Social Marketing + Behavioral Architecture  ·  Budget: €10,000  ·  Institution: University of Galway

The Context

A behavioral research initiative targeting Galway City’s natural “Blue Spaces” (coastal and river resources). The objective was to increase public utilization of these spaces to improve mental and physical health, utilizing a €10,000 Systems Social Marketing budget.

The Phenomenological Audit

I mapped the ecosystem using PESTLE analysis, Stakeholder Mapping (Galway City Council, HSE), and Causal Loop Diagrams. The data revealed that “Poor Infrastructure” and “Lack of Knowledge” were creating negative feedback loops. Survey data showed 15% of citizens cited “work priority” and 10% cited “reluctance to change” as primary barriers. People knew nature was good for them, but failed to act.

Behavioral Diagnoses

Temporal Surrender

Citizens were consistently surrendering their long-term health goals to short-term work stress.

Default Architecture Failure

Sedentary indoor environments were the default. Engaging with outdoor resources required an “active choice” that generated cognitive friction.

The Intention-Action Gap

Traditional marketing relied on logical health arguments (System 2), which failed to overcome the friction of changing established daily routines.

Interventions Designed

Choice Architecture (Defaulting): Proposed partnering with major local employers to make “Walking Meetings” along the River Corrib the default option for internal 1-on-1s. This removed the friction of having to actively “choose” exercise over work.

Social Proof & Mimetic Influence: Because 38% of respondents heavily valued “social relations,” the campaign messaging shifted from “Nature is healthy” to “Your neighbors are already at Salthill.” Using local micro-influencers created a mimetic effect, nudging people to mirror the behavior to remain part of the social fabric.

Micro-Incentives: Designed short-term reward loops for 15-minute coastal walks to provide the immediate gratification the brain craves, bypassing Temporal Surrender.

Impact

Delivered a comprehensive, highly targeted behavioral blueprint to the Social Science Department, providing actionable, low-cost nudges to break negative causal loops and drive sustained behavioral adoption of public resources.

Case File · 04 · Brand Strategy / D2C Ayurvedic Adoption-Abandonment Gap Bridged · Brand Equity Rebuilt

The “Millennial Mom” & The Relatability Heuristic — Rich Herbals Brand Strategy

Scope: Behavioral Brand Strategy + Digital Adoption Architecture  ·  Client: Rich Herbals (Ayurvedic D2C)

The Context

Rich Herbals, an established Ayurvedic brand, faced stagnant growth. They were trapped in generic retail transactions where customers purchased ingredients based on price, not brand loyalty.

The Phenomenological Audit

The brand lacked a sustainable digital adoption architecture. For their target demographic—the “Millennial Mom”—choosing wellness products is a high-stakes decision heavily filtered through social proof and risk aversion.

Behavioral Diagnoses

The Commensurability Trap

Selling generic herbal names trapped the brand in a “Market Norm” (price-sensitive comparison), leading to immediate abandonment for cheaper alternatives.

Decision Paralysis

The “Big Chemical” stigma created a mimetic threat to the consumer’s identity as a protective, informed parent.

Interventions Designed

Identity Signaling: Rebranded from a generic provider to a “Specialized Solution Architect.” Redesigned packaging allowed the user to signal her identity as a conscious consumer.

The Trust Cascade (Relatability Heuristic): Deployed a micro-influencer strategy using local “Mom-Bloggers” rather than polished models. This leveraged the Relatability Heuristic—when a mother observed peers vetting the product, the brain perceived a “Safe Haven,” bypassing risk-aversion filters.

Impact

Successfully bridged the Adoption-Abandonment Gap, transitioning the brand from a low-margin generic retailer into a high-equity specialized provider with a digitally-connected, loyal community.

N=1 Self Case Study: Applied Adherence

I don't just study behavioral retention; I engineer it in my own life.

As a competitive endurance athlete — currently training for the HYROX Pro Division and full-distance triathlons — I treat my own biology as a complex, high-friction system.

The fitness industry thrives on the "Adoption-Abandonment Gap." Most athletes start training based on Mimetic Motivation (social mirroring) but inevitably hit Temporal Surrender when the cognitive and physical load becomes too high to maintain.

To survive the volume required for hybrid and endurance racing, I have had to map my own friction points and design personal choice architecture that bypasses motivation entirely. I engineer my training environment with rigid default systems and immediate feedback loops, forcing the transition from a highly volatile "social biological reality check" into a compounding, unbreakable habit.

When I build retention strategies for brands, I am applying the exact same behavioral adherence protocols that I use to get myself to the finish line.

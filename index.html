About Me

"I am a behavioral scientist and product strategist. I do not design interfaces — I design choice architecture."

For the past 8.5+ years, I have audited complex systems — from high-governance organizational protocols to D2C digital ecosystems — to understand why users fail to adopt systems and how to engineer sustained behavioral adherence.

My practice is built on a single conviction: most product failures are not feature failures. They are behavioral failures. The interface shipped, but the choice architecture was never engineered. Users arrived, attempted adoption, and surrendered — not because the product lacked functionality, but because the cognitive cost of sustained use exceeded what the system was designed to support.

I work at the intersection of behavioral economics, cognitive science, and systems design. I do not hand off decks and disappear. I audit, diagnose, intervene, and measure — staying in the engagement until the behavior actually changes.

§ II

The Frameworks

Two core operating principles that define how I diagnose behavioral failure
and engineer sustained adoption.

01 — The Adoption-Abandonment Gap / The critical product vulnerability

The critical vulnerability in any product lifecycle. I map the exact cognitive friction points where users successfully adopt a platform (System 1 thinking) but abandon it when the cognitive load or process friction exceeds their mental reserve (System 2 overload). Most product teams mistake this gap for a motivation problem or a UX polish problem. It is neither. It is a choice architecture failure — and it is entirely engineerable.

↓    the mechanism by which adoption collapses into abandonment

02 — Temporal Surrender / The abandonment mechanism

A behavioral state where users hyper-adapt to a new system or subculture — intensive fitness models, complex enterprise software, high-stakes exam preparation — only to inevitably drop off when the cost of maintenance burns out their cognitive bandwidth. I design interventions to move users from high-volatility hyper-adaptation to compounding, sustainable routines. The goal is not peak engagement. The goal is behavioral durability.

Case File · 01 · Organizational / High-Governance ▲ 55% Behavioral Adherence Rate

Engineering Adherence in a High-Governance Environment — Sameekshnam Project

Scope: Organizational Behavioral Audit + Adherence Protocol Design  ·  Client: Indian Paramilitary Unit  ·  Role: Project Manager, Ammachi Labs

The Context

Managed a large-scale organizational intervention for a high-governance Indian paramilitary unit. The objective was to implement new operational and behavioral protocols without disrupting the rigid, high-pressure daily functions of the unit.

The Phenomenological Audit

High-stakes, rigid environments inherently resist new processes. An audit of the daily routines revealed that introducing any new protocol required active cognitive effort, pulling focus away from critical operational duties — triggering immediate Status Quo Bias at the unit level.

Behavioral Diagnoses

Status Quo Bias

In high-stress settings, human operators default to established routines. Any new behavioral requirement is perceived as operational friction and quickly abandoned.

Cognitive Overload

New protocols demanded active decision-making capacity that was already fully allocated to critical operational duties under high-pressure conditions.

Process Drift

Without constant enforcement, the gap between the prescribed protocol and actual on-ground behavior widens until full abandonment occurs.

Intervention Designed

Systemic Adherence Protocols: Rather than relying on motivation or enforcement, program delivery was engineered to seamlessly integrate into the unit's existing operational architecture. New behaviors were embedded directly into standard feedback loops, removing the need for active decision-making at the point of execution.

Measured Impact

55% user adherence rate achieved — successfully enforcing sustained behavioral change and operational compliance within an incredibly high-pressure, low-flexibility environment.

Case File · 02 · EdTech / Competitive Exam Platform ▲ 55% Practice Adherence · ▲ 10% Active Practice Hours

Mitigating the Adoption-Abandonment Gap in High-Stakes Prep

Scope: Behavioral Audit + Intervention Design  ·  Platform: Premier UPSC/NID EdTech  ·  Client under NDA

The Context

A premier Indian EdTech platform for competitive exams (UPSC/NID) was experiencing a systemic "Implementation Choke-Point." While initial adoption was high — 90% video consumption — active practice execution (mock tests and sketches) dropped by 65% after 30 days.

The Phenomenological Audit

I audited the student journey to understand the friction between consuming content and executing practice. The data revealed a shift from active engagement to "Passive Surrender" — where users abandoned high-cognitive-load tasks for the psychological comfort of passive watching, which provided an illusion of progress with zero mimetic risk.

Behavioral Diagnoses

Mimetic Threat

Public leaderboards created intense social pressure. Students viewed poor initial practice performance as an existential threat to their projected persona as a serious candidate.

Temporal Surrender

Hyperbolic discounting drove users to surrender long-term exam goals for the immediate psychological comfort of passive consumption — zero mimetic risk, full illusion of progress.

Executive System Bypass

The platform architecture allowed users to satisfy the need to "feel productive" without executing the high-friction behaviors that actually produce learning outcomes.

Interventions Designed

De-Commensurability (Norm Shifting): Replaced market-normed leaderboards with "Social Norm" process badges for the first 60 days, neutralizing the mimetic rivalry that triggered surrender.

Action-Locked Content (Adherence Engineering): Passive videos remained locked until a low-stakes "Micro-Task" (a 5-minute sketch or single practice question) was completed — forcing the Executive System to override the impulsive flight response before content access was granted.

Measured Impact (Client Name Withheld — NDA)

55% adherence rate to daily practice protocols during high-stress periods  ·  +10% increase in measurable "Active Practice Hours"  ·  Direct increase in platform LTV

Case File · 03 · Public Policy / Behavioral Research Behavioral Blueprint Delivered · NUI Galway / HSE

Behavioral Nudging for Public Resource Adoption — NUI Galway Blue Spaces Initiative

Scope: Systems Social Marketing + Behavioral Architecture  ·  Budget: €10,000  ·  Institution: University of Galway

The Context

A behavioral research initiative targeting Galway City's natural "Blue Spaces" (coastal and river resources). The objective was to increase public utilization of these spaces to improve mental and physical health outcomes, using a €10,000 Systems Social Marketing budget with Galway City Council and HSE as key stakeholders.

The Phenomenological Audit

Ecosystem mapped via PESTLE analysis, Stakeholder Mapping, and Causal Loop Diagrams. Survey data revealed: 15% cited "work priority" and 10% cited "reluctance to change" as primary barriers. Citizens knew nature was beneficial — but consistently failed to act on that knowledge.

Behavioral Diagnoses

Temporal Surrender

Citizens consistently surrendered long-term health goals to short-term work stress. The sedentary indoor default required no active decision — it was the path of least cognitive resistance.

Default Architecture Failure

Engaging with outdoor resources required an active choice that generated friction. No infrastructure existed to make the healthy behavior the default behavior.

Intention-Action Gap

Traditional marketing used logical health arguments (System 2) that failed to overcome the friction of changing routines anchored in System 1 automaticity.

Interventions Designed

Choice Architecture (Defaulting): Proposed partnering with major local employers to make "Walking Meetings" along the River Corrib the default option for internal 1-on-1s — removing the friction of actively choosing exercise over work.

Social Proof & Mimetic Influence: Shifted campaign messaging from "Nature is healthy" to "Your neighbors are already at Salthill." 38% of respondents heavily valued social relations — local micro-influencers created a mimetic effect nudging behavioral mirroring to remain part of the social fabric.

Micro-Incentives: Designed short-term reward loops for 15-minute coastal walks to provide the immediate gratification the brain craves, bypassing Temporal Surrender.

Impact

Delivered a comprehensive, highly targeted behavioral blueprint to the NUI Galway Social Science Department — actionable, low-cost nudges to break negative causal loops and drive sustained behavioral adoption of public resources.

Case File · 04 · Brand Strategy / D2C Ayurvedic Adoption-Abandonment Gap Bridged · Brand Equity Rebuilt

The "Millennial Mom" & The Relatability Heuristic — Rich Herbals Brand Strategy

Scope: Behavioral Brand Strategy + Digital Adoption Architecture  ·  Client: Rich Herbals (Ayurvedic D2C)

The Context

Rich Herbals, an established Ayurvedic brand, faced stagnant growth. They were trapped in generic retail transactions where customers purchased ingredients on price, not brand loyalty. Target demographic: the "Millennial Mom" — for whom choosing wellness products is a high-stakes decision heavily filtered through social proof and risk aversion.

The Phenomenological Audit

The brand lacked a sustainable digital adoption architecture. Audit revealed that selling generic herbal names locked the brand into a "Market Norm" — price-sensitive comparison shopping — triggering immediate abandonment for cheaper alternatives at every purchase decision point.

Behavioral Diagnoses

The Commensurability Trap

Generic herbal category names made the brand directly comparable to cheaper alternatives. Price became the only differentiator — a race to the bottom.

Decision Paralysis

The "Big Chemical" stigma created a mimetic threat to the consumer's identity as a protective, informed parent — triggering avoidance rather than committed purchase.

Trust Deficit

No social proof architecture in the digital ecosystem. Polished brand models failed to activate the "Safe Haven" signal that drives commitment in high-stakes parenting decisions.

Interventions Designed

Identity Signaling: Rebranded from generic provider to "Specialized Solution Architect." Redesigned packaging allowed the user to signal her identity as a conscious, informed consumer — activating identity-behavior congruence at the point of purchase.

The Trust Cascade (Relatability Heuristic): Deployed a micro-influencer strategy using local "Mom-Bloggers" rather than polished models. When a mother observed peers vetting the product, the brain perceived a "Safe Haven" signal, bypassing risk-aversion filters and accelerating purchase commitment.

Impact

Successfully bridged the Adoption-Abandonment Gap — transitioning the brand from a low-margin generic retailer into a high-equity specialized provider with a digitally-connected, loyal community.
